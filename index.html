10 Reasons Why People Management Is Important for Organizations

People Management a strategic approach to managing an organization’s workforce’s main importance is enhancing employee engagement, productivity, and overall organizational success. Effective people management ensures that employees have boosted motivation and productivity, Idea sharing, aligning with the company’s goals to achieve business performance.

Key aspects of people management include fostering a positive work environment, providing growth opportunities, and recognizing employee contributions. Research published in the Journal of Applied Psychology indicates that a positive work environment significantly reduces stress and enhances employee satisfaction and productivity.
A People Manager plays a crucial role in implementing these strategies and maintaining a healthy workplace culture.

The effects of bad people management can lead to low morale, high turnover rates, and decreased productivity. Understanding the importance of people in an organization highlights the need for strong people management practices.

People Management Roles involve tasks such as recruitment, training, performance evaluation, and conflict resolution. First-time managers can greatly benefit from understanding the importance of people management as it helps them build effective teams and navigate their new responsibilities.

Developing people management skills like communication, empathy, and leadership is essential for anyone in a managerial position.
The list below is 10 core importance of effective people management and organizational practices that prioritize employee engagement and development.

  1. Achieves business performance
  2. Boosts motivation and productivity
  3. Cultivates Idea sharing
  4. Encourages Collaboration
  5. Facilitates communication
  6. Creates an improved working environment
  7. Fosters employee development
  8. Enhances retention
  9. Resolves conflicts
  10. Promotes employee well-being

1. Achieves Business Performance

Business performance is a company’s ability to profit from resources and meet objectives. People management is crucial for achieving high business performance. Aligning employee goals with organizational objectives boosts productivity and profitability. When employees understand their roles and feel valued, they will contribute positively.

Cai-Hui (Veronica) Lin’s meta-analysis at Queen’s Management School found that work engagement explains 19% more variance in task performance than job satisfaction and commitment. Effective people management helps managers drive teams toward goals. In contrast, poor people management can harm business performance and lead to financial losses.

2. Boosts motivation and productivity

Motivation is driving employees to reach goals, while productivity is measuring how effectively efforts create valuable results. People management enhances motivation and productivity by fostering an environment where employees feel valued and inspired to perform their best. Motivated employees are more productive, directly boosting company success.

Harvard Business Review reports that highly engaged employees are 50% more likely to exceed performance expectations, underscoring the role of people management in sustaining motivation and productivity. Conversely, poor management leads to disengagement and reduced productivity. Motivated employees also frequently share innovative ideas and SOPs, aiding team cohesion and organizational growth.

3. Cultivates Idea sharing

Idea sharing is cultivated by fostering open communication and innovation. When employees feel safe to share ideas, it sparks creative solutions and process improvements.

A study by MIT Sloan found that companies encouraging idea-sharing are 3.5 times more likely to outperform competitors. Without effective people management, employees may hesitate to share ideas, stifling creativity and growth.

4. Encourages Collaboration

Encouraging collaboration is vital in people management, fostering teamwork, enhancing communication, and driving collective problem-solving. Collaboration boosts job satisfaction and creates a sense of belonging among employees.

According to Deloitte’s report, “Delivering on the Promise of Digital Collaboration” (2018) by Tharani Jegatheeswaran and Phillip Mottram, 73% of employees who collaborate are more productive, and 60% are more innovative. Harvard Business Review also notes that collaborative environments improve performance and retention, highlighting how effective people management enhances collaboration a critical factor for business growth and a positive work culture.

Poor people management, in contrast, lowers morale, reduces productivity, and increases turnover. Integrating collaboration effectively strengthens communication, a key driver of business growth.

5. Facilitates Communication

Communication in people management is sharing information and feedback between managers and employees, ensuring mutual understanding, alignment, and teamwork. People management establishes clear channels for communication and effective information flow, reducing misunderstandings and boosting efficiency.

A study by Towers Watson found that companies with high employee engagement including effective communication experience 19% lower turnover than those with low engagement. This underscores the importance of clear communication in driving organizational growth. Poor communication leads to misunderstandings, low engagement, conflicts, and reduced performance. Effective communication fosters open dialogue, resolving conflicts and strengthening relationships between employees and managers.

6. Resolves Conflicts

Conflicts are disagreements, misunderstandings, or clashes between individuals or groups within an organization, which can hinder collaboration, productivity, and overall performance. Effective conflict management is a vital skill for managers, enabling them to address disputes constructively and promote a positive, productive work environment.

People management resolves conflicts by facilitating open communication, understanding different perspectives, mediating disputes, and implementing fair solutions to maintain harmony and productivity.

The ability to resolve conflicts fosters collaboration, reduces stress and turnover, and sustains organizational productivity. An AMA study found that conflict resolution training reduces absenteeism by 10-20% and increases productivity by 15-30%. Unresolved conflicts lower morale, reduce productivity, increase turnover, and create a toxic work environment, all damaging to organizational performance and employee satisfaction. Effective conflict resolution enhances the workplace atmosphere.

7. Creates an Improved Working Environment

The working environment is the physical, psychological, and social conditions influencing employee productivity, well-being, and job satisfaction. People management enhances the working environment by addressing employee needs and fostering a positive workplace culture.

A supportive environment boosts job satisfaction and reduces stress. Research by Harvard Business Review shows that a positive work environment increases employee satisfaction by 24% and productivity by 31%, supported by a study from Oxford University Business School. Poor people management creates a negative environment, lowering employee satisfaction and effectiveness, which negatively impacts organizational success. Improving the working environment boosts employee well-being, productivity, and job satisfaction.

8. Promotes Employee Well-being

Employee well-being is the overall health and satisfaction of employees, people management plays a pivotal role in fostering employee well-being through supportive practices and a positive work environment. People management promotes well-being by creating a conducive work environment, offering development opportunities, and addressing employee needs.

Well-being leads to higher job satisfaction, better performance, reduced absenteeism, and a more engaged workforce. A 2010 HBR report, “What’s the Hard Return on Employee Wellness Programs?” by Leonard L. Berry, Ann M. Mirabito, and William B. Baun, reveals that companies with strong wellness programs see returns of up to $3 for every $1 invested, enhancing both health and productivity. Neglecting well-being results in lower job satisfaction, reduced productivity, higher absenteeism, and increased turnover, all negatively impacting organizational performance. Effective people management fosters well-being and development, driving organizational growth.

9. Fosters Employee Development

Employee development is focusing on improving skills and advancing careers, enhancing job performance and organizational success. People management fosters development by offering learning opportunities, coaching, and support, enabling employees to improve and progress, benefiting both employees and the organization.

A 2019 LinkedIn Learning report found that 94% of employees would stay longer at companies that invest in career development. Without effective management, employees may feel stagnant and unmotivated. Companies that neglect development limit growth opportunities, reducing motivation, productivity, and innovation. Effective development increases retention by providing career advancement, boosting satisfaction and loyalty.

10. Enhances Retention

Retention is the ability to keep employees engaged and committed to the organization, ensuring long-term contributions to success. Effective people management enhances retention by fostering a positive work environment, offering growth opportunities, and addressing employee needs.

Retention reduces hiring and training costs, preserves organizational knowledge, boosts morale, and ensures stability. A manager with strong people management skills like effective communication, empathy, and emotional intelligence, enhances retention through effective practices.

Research by the Work Institute (2018) shows that 77% of employee turnover is prevented with effective people management, emphasizing the importance of growth opportunities in retaining employees. Poor people management, however, results in high turnover, increased hiring costs, and workforce instability. Effective people management improves retention, reduces recruitment costs, and ensures business continuity.

Why are People managers important?

People managers are important because they guide, support, and reward teams, driving productivity, engagement, and retention. This fosters employee motivation and accomplishment. According to Wellable, highly engaged employees contribute to a 21% rise in profitability and a 41% drop in absenteeism, underscoring “reasons Why Organizations Need People Managers.” as demonstrated by Louis Gerstner’s strategic leadership at IBM and Satya Nadella’s focus on innovation and collaboration at Microsoft, both of which greatly boosted company performance.

What is People Management?

People management is the process of leading, supporting, and developing employees by providing clear direction, resources, development opportunities, feedback, recognition, conflict resolution, and a positive work culture to achieve organizational goals. Effective people management creates a productive, engaged, and satisfied workforce, driving organizational performance and success.

Why is management Important?

Management is important because it ensures organizational goals are achieved by effectively coordinating and overseeing employee activities. Good management aligns employees’ efforts with the organization’s strategic objectives. A 2019 Predictive Index study found that 60% of employees who rate their managers as “good” or “world-class” show higher engagement and productivity, highlighting the value of effective management in creating a supportive, motivating work environment.

What are the Effects of Bad People Management?

The main effects of “Bad people management” are high turnover, low morale, increased stress, reduced productivity, a toxic work environment, and stifled innovation. Bad people management results in financial losses, wasted recruitment and training resources, potential legal issues, and a damaged reputation, ultimately harming employee well-being and organizational success. A report by The Predictive Index shows 47% of employees have quit due to a bad manager. Research from the Harvard Business Review also links poor leadership particularly lacking emotional intelligence and organization with higher employee stress, underscoring management quality’s role in retention.

Are Managers Responsible for the Success or Failure of a Company?

Yes, managers are fundamentally responsible for the success or failure of a company because they make key decisions, set the organizational culture, and influence employee performance and engagement. The Harvard Business Review article “The Frontline Advantage” by Fred Hassan (May 2011) notes that frontline managers are accountable for up to 60% of a company’s operational success. Effective managers foster employee morale, increase productivity, and create a positive work environment by providing clear direction, support, and recognition. Conversely, a bad manager can lead to low employee morale, high turnover rates, and poor team cohesion, ultimately impacting organizational performance and sustainability.

What are people management roles?

People management roles are overseeing and guiding employees to ensure they are productive, motivated, and aligned with the organization’s goals. “Effective Human Resource Roles” are crucial in leading and developing employees to meet organizational goals. These roles encompass recruitment, training, performance management, and compensation. Strong people management fosters a positive work environment, supports professional growth, and plays a vital role in Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging (DEIB) initiatives, promoting a fair and inclusive workplace. For first-time managers, understanding these roles is essential to lead effectively, drive productivity, and boost employee satisfaction.

What are some people management skills?

People management skills are the abilities and attributes necessary to lead, motivate, and support employees to achieve organizational goals. “People management skills for effective leadership” include competencies such as effective communication, conflict resolution, empathy, team building, and constructive feedback. These skills are crucial for fostering a collaborative and productive work environment. According to Josh Bersin, CEO of the Josh Bersin Academy, the future of work has little to do with technology, AI, or algorithms. It’s all about people, organizations, and how we manage people within these organizations,” underscoring the essential role of people management skills in shaping the future of business success.
